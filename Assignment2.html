Worldwide Happiness Scores

The score is an index derived from the responses to the "Gallup World Survey", this survey is conducted in each country and has a sample size of around 1000 individuals. It asks participants various life questions which relate to the country they live in, World Happiness Report then summarises the results into six factors being Gross Domestic Profit , life expectancy, generosity, social support , freedom and corruption and use these to give each country a single value where higher values reflect a happier country and lower values being a less happier one

This page shows a collection of visualisations to show the difference in Happiness scores between regions, and how Australia has performed througout the years. A handful of visualisations will also show the trends and relationships between the main factors that go into calculating the score.

Composition?

Happiness Scores are derived from the results obtained from each of the 6 factors, and summed together using a weighting system Gallup.

The visualisation to the right shows the score of a selection of 9 countries, using the 4 highest weighted factors, where for example Freedom relates to a individuals level of choice in making decisions in their lives. the sum of factors is not the happiness score of the country but rather cumulative score of the 4 factors. Users can select the factors they want to compare using drop down box, and graph is sorted in descending order for easy comparison.

Generally "Western" countries dominated the scores, however its interesting to see how countries like Bulgaria can gain a higher score than China, even though their GDP is much smaller at 84.32 billion in 2022, while China had 20.25 trillion, however all factors are weighted with other measurements, hence even with this big difference Bulgaria had a better score.

A Comparison

Now having insight with how the scores are calculated, lets see how Australia's score compares with the rest of the world, And if certain regions perform better than others.

The line chart on the left compares australia's Happiness scores with the world and 4 other countries from different regions from the years 2015-2022. Click on the country drop down box to see line trend of one country, and also toggle with the slider to set boundary on year range.

The boxplot on the right, aims to show the distribution of Happiest Scores depending on the region, Since there is a variable number of countries between the regions and a variance of scores, users will be able to see how a regions data is skewed, and with using region selection drop box, readers will be able to investigate the shape of the plots more as the axis will also zoom in.

A comparison between Australia and countries of various regions is good as it shows a more complete picture of the significance of Australia's score. Australia has high scores in all the major factors such as in 2022 received a weighted score of 1.9 for Gross Domestic Profit and 1.203 for social support in 2022. which can be explained by Australia's high supply of raw minerals per capita and its health care and social support systems such as Medicare and Centrelink, which both of whom provide more assurances to population and improve happiness scores. While Australia' s scores have remained relatively stable, countries like Ecuador have dipped since 2019 whereas in the same year Iraq has improved.

The boxplots are interesting as while Australia New Zealand have a very small box plot due to only 2 countries being in the region, with similar scores. Middle East and North Africa was extremely variable as, the region included countries like Israel and Bahrain who have high socio economic statutes and well developed, but also countries like Lebanon and Yemen who have ongoing conflicts and recessions.

A General Distribution

The Heatmap to the right, plots the records of all the countries from the years 2015-2022, depending on their score for freedom of choice, which relates to individuals level of choice in making decisions in their lives, and a countries happiness score, from this the user can deduce which pair of scores are most likely to be attained and the general distribution of how many countries have both a high freedom of choice and happiness score, as Freedom of choice is not as an important factor as the others detailed above.
